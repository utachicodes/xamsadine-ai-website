About Dr. Ahmed Lo

Early Life and Education

Dr. Ahmed Lo was born in 1955 in Tawfekh near Touba. He memorized the Quran in Ndame, in the Louga region of Senegal. He learned basic Islamic knowledge first in Touba and then in Saint-Louis. He worked as a teacher from 1975 to 1979 in Ndakhar-Mbaye near Gaye-Mékhé and then in Dièlerlou Sylla near Louga.

During this time, he developed an excellent level in Arabic, allowing him to write beautiful poems. However, he felt the need to learn more about his religion despite financial constraints.

Journey for Knowledge

An adventurer in search of knowledge, he traveled with friends through Mali, Côte d'Ivoire, Benin, Nigeria, and Egypt, where he worked briefly to fund his pilgrimage to Umrah in Saudi Arabia in 1980, his final destination after eight months of travel.

Unable to enroll in university, he decided to write a poem about his goal of studying Islam, which he presented to the rector of the University of Medina, Dr. Abdullah Zaïd, after a prayer. Moved by the style of his poetry, the rector and his staff unanimously decided to admit him to the university after verifying the work was indeed his.

Academic Achievements

Dr. Lo began his studies in 1980-81 at the age of 25, passing the entrance exam to secondary school in three months, and obtaining his baccalaureate three years later. He entered the faculty of hadith, where he earned his degree after four years, with a thesis on "The Efforts of Scholars Towards the Six Books."

He was selected for his oral examination by a university jury to pursue his doctorate, focusing on the sensitive topic of "The Sanctification of Saints in Light of Sufi Doctrine."

Community Involvement

Dr. Lo has been involved in various community outreach programs, helping to educate individuals about Islamic teachings and practices. His approachable demeanor and extensive knowledge make him a valuable resource for anyone looking to deepen their understanding of Islam.

He has also successfully resolved sensitive diplomatic issues through personal initiatives, without expecting any compensation from the Senegalese state.
